Educational Technology Tools Menu

As an educator, choosing the right tools and strategies to achieve your learning outcomes can be challenging, but doing so is essential to student success. This guide is designed to help you align educational technologies with specific learning types, supporting student engagement and deeper learning throughout your course.

As you explore this guide, consider the following questions:

  • What types of learning and engagement do you want students to experience in order to achieve your course’s intended outcomes?
  • What challenges might prevent your students from achieving these outcomes?
  • How do you want your students to engage with new concepts in your class?

Now that you’ve reflected on your learning goals and student needs, take some time to review the Learning Types (Laurillard, 2012) below. Each section is interactive. Click on a learning type to explore a curated list of recommended tools that support that specific kind of learning.

Use this resource to experiment, reflect, and adapt your teaching strategies to create meaningful and impactful learning experiences.

Learning Types (Click on a learning type to review the tools we recommend)

Reading, Listening & Watching
Students learn by engaging with content through reading, listening, or watching. These activities help them absorb foundational knowledge and set the stage for deeper exploration. When paired with prompts, such as pause-and-reflect questions or note-taking strategies, these modalities become more active, helping students process information and prepare to apply it in other contexts.
Collaborating
Students learn through collaboration when they work together to build knowledge, solve problems, or co-create products. This kind of learning often involves shared documents, group projects, or problem-based tasks that require collective decision-making. Clear roles, purposeful interaction, and structured goals ensure the process strengthens understanding while promoting teamwork and accountability.
Discussing
Discussion-based learning encourages students to articulate their ideas, respond to questions, and engage with diverse perspectives. Whether in class debates, peer dialogues, or online forums, discussion helps learners test thinking, refine understanding, and build deeper connections to course content.
Researching
Students learn through researching by exploring and evaluating information to build evidence-based understanding. They might investigate case studies, compare sources, or analyze data sets to draw conclusions. This type of learning fosters curiosity, critical thinking, and a deeper engagement with the ideas and materials that shape the discipline.
Practicing & Assessing
Practice and assessment help students develop skills through application and feedback. Activities such as simulations, peer reviews, or quizzes give learners the opportunity to refine their approach and improve over time. Feedback, whether from peers, instructors, or the activity itself, guides growth and builds confidence.
Presenting
Through presenting, students consolidate their learning by explaining what they’ve understood and how they’ve applied it. Whether through class presentations, video pitches, or written showcases, this type of learning pushes students to organize their thoughts clearly, make their reasoning visible, and reflect on their progress.
